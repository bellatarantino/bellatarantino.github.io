COURSE CONTENT

About the Course

This is a 6-week preparatory course for the AMC 8 exam. The AMC 8 exam s a math contest organized by the Mathematical Association of America (MAA). It is primarily for students in Grade 8 and below. High-scoring students have the opportunity to qualify for the AMC 10 exam, which is primarily meant for students between Grade 8 and Grade 10. The goal of this course is to provide students a mathematical foundation for the AMC 8, expose them to competitive math test-taking strategies, and prepare them through both theory and practice.

Curriculum

This is a 6-week program, and the curriculum is tailored towards students who have not prepared for the AMC 8 before. Sessions will ideally be 90 minutes (with a short break) but can be adjusted in length and frequency to match student needs. To help me provide a more tailored curriculum, students can fill out the form attached on the sign-up page. Depending on student needs, the course can be extended, and sessions can be lengthened. You can find the preliminary syllabus here.

Resources

One of the leading sources on American mathematics competitions is the Art of Problem Solving (AoPS), an organization which develops courses and books preparing students for competitive mathematics, physics, programming, and more. For this course, we will be utilizing "Volume 1: The Basics" by AoPS. This book provides a good balance of both theory and practice and was written by past competition winners, writers, and educators. The table of contents should be visible to the right and is also available on the official website.

You can purchase the book and its solutions here. Ideally, students should not have access to the solutions until I release them. While purchasing the workbook is encouraged, it is not required for this course. I will provide relevant chapters, exercises, and solutions as needed. Students can expect to have access to the content needed for this course electronically, but no more than that.

Diagnostics

  1. Diagnostic Exam. This is the 2005 AMC 8 exam, which we will use as a baseline for the students' performance. Please have the student take the exam under real test conditions, as outlined in the form. Once they submit their answers to the form, they will automatically be graded. Students can expect 1 exam per week, and, by the end of the 6 weeks, we will be able to qualitatively describe their progress! This will take 40 minutes to complete, and about 5 minutes to upload their answers.
  2. Self-Evaluation. This is a short self-evaluation that asks the students to rate their comfort on topics potentially covered in this course. Because the course will ideally be tailored to the class, having the students indicate their levels of comfort with relevant topics will allow me to make the course more or less rigorous dependent on their needs.
