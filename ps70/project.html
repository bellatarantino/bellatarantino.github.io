Final Project

Background

One of the most popular training tools to increase hand and finger strength in climbing is known as a hangboard (or fingerboard). Hangboards are oft made of plastic or wood and have a variety of holds to hang from and train on. By slowly increasing the duration of a hang or the shallowness of the holds you hang from, you can train and track improvement in finger and hand strength.

Motivation

Tracking your progress in climbing can be hard as the difficulty between grades increases. Though finger strength is not the most important factor in your climbing journey, it can be a good indicator of improvement. You often hear the phrase, "Numbers aren't important." But to that I say, "Why does my self worth rely entirely on them, then?" How can you obtain a more accurate measure of your strength and also get your fix of sweet, sweet numerical validation?

Proposal

My final project would be a pressure-sensitive hangboard that measures the magnitude of force each hand — or finger, depending on the hold — is applying to the board. This could help quantitatively identify strength imbalances from hand to hand or finger to finger and help track improvement more concretely. Disclaimer: I discovered that this product does, in fact, exist already — the Climbro smart board — but it's nearly $800.
