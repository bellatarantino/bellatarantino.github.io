Completed Topic
The discriminant. The discriminant is used to determine how many distinct solutions (0, 1, 2) a quadratic has. It is also used to determine whether these solutions will be real, imaginary, rational, or irrational. To feel that you are confident in using the discriminant, you should be able to, one, recognize when it is applicable; two, know what it is; three, be able to evaluate it; four, be able to distinguish between how many solutions a quadratic will have; five, be able to tell whether solutions are real or imaginary; six, be able to tell whether solutions are rational or irrational.
Mixtures. Mixtures are solutions that are composed of multiple materials. You should be able to figure out, given two solutions, the concentration of certain materials in a mixed solution. You should feel confident in identifying the exact weight or quantity of one material within the solution, given its concentration.
Rearranging and creating equations. Given a word problem or a relationship, you should be able to create a corresponding equation or system of equations that you can solve. Given an equation, you should be able to solve for any variable and write any variable in terms of another.
Solving complicated systems of equations and inequalities. You should be able to recognize when an inequality is necessary in a word problem, how to set it up, and how to solve it. Similarly, you should be able to recognize when a system of equations is necessary, and how to solve it, especially if it is a complicated quadratic.
